The social initiative of K.J. Somaiya College of engineering, named Parvaah, works each year with a purpose of providing to the underprivileged and oppressed. This time around, Parvaah is on a mission to establish a Zero Waste Community, wherein constant efforts are being made for reducing, reusing and recycling the waste generated and contributing back to the society.

Beautification Drive

KJSCE Parvaah conducted a beautification drive at Matunga Station Road in collaboration with KJSCE Insignia. The outcome of the drive was a beautiful mural of all the frontline covid workers who made these tough times easy for us. This activity was conducted under Project Mumbai’s initiative called ‘Salaam Rakshak’, where public gratitude arts were being painted at public places as a tribute to India’s COVID warriors.

Mangrove Clean Up

In collaboration with Beach Please India founded by Malhar Kalambe, Team Parvaah volunteered for the Mangrove Clean Up Drive in Airoli. Our team assisted in cleaning the mangrove patches in Navi Mumbai, which are basically the flood barriers of Mumbai.

Orphanage Visit

Holding up to the rationales of Diwali, KJSCE Parvaah successfully carried out its goal of spreading positivity and happiness with the initiative of an orphanage visit to celebrate humanity! The visit was made at the Abundant Life Miseries where our team interacted with the kids and filled their hearts with excitement and hearts.

Beach Clean Up

Parvaah along with Mr. Afroz Shah proudly joined hands to help clean up Versova Beach. Parvaah initiated this journey on World Coastal Clean Up Day. Together with a multitude of students of KJSCE, Parvaah helped to change the sorry state of the Versova beach.
